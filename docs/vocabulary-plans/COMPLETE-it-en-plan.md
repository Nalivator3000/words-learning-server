## Italian-Specific Features

### Advantages for English Speakers

1. **Phonetic spelling** - Pronounce as you read
2. **Latin cognates** - Many similar words (musica, importante)
3. **Regular patterns** - More predictable than French
4. **Clear vowels** - Each vowel has one main sound

### Key Grammar Points

1. **Gender** - All nouns masculine/feminine
2. **Articles** - Il/lo/la, un/uno/una
3. **Verb conjugations** - Three groups (-are, -ere, -ire)
4. **Subjunctive** - Less extensive than French, more than Spanish
5. **Double consonants** - Pronunciation critical
6. **Tu/Lei** - Informal/formal distinction

### Common Challenges

1. **False friends** - camera ‚â† camera (room), parents ‚â† parenti (relatives)
2. **Double consonants** - Change meaning: pena (sorrow) vs penna (pen)
3. **Irregular verbs** - essere, avere, andare, fare very common
4. **Prepositions** - a, di, da, in, con combine with articles
