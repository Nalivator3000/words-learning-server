# French → English Vocabulary Plan

**Target Audience:** English speakers learning French
**Difficulty Level:** Medium-High (complex grammar, pronunciation)
**Market Size:** Large (popular among English speakers)
**Priority:** HIGH (third most popular after Spanish, German)

---

## Total Target: ~10,800 words

### Key French-Specific Features

1. **Gender System** - All nouns masculine/feminine
2. **Liaison & Elision** - Pronunciation rules
3. **Verb Conjugations** - Complex system (être, avoir, irregular verbs)
4. **Subjunctive Mood** - Required for fluency
5. **Formal/Informal** - Tu vs Vous distinction
6. **Nasal Vowels** - Unique pronunciation
7. **Silent Letters** - Many unpronounced endings
8. **Articles** - Le, la, les, un, une, des, du, de la

### Level Breakdown (same structure as Spanish/English)

**A1:** 800 words - Basic communication
**A2:** 1,000 words - Elementary topics
**B1:** 1,200 words - Intermediate conversation
**B2:** 1,800 words - Advanced topics
**C1:** 2,500 words - Professional/academic
**C2:** 3,500 words - Near-native mastery

### Priority Grammar & Vocabulary

- Passé composé vs Imparfait (past tenses)
- Subjunctive mood (le subjonctif)
- Partitive articles (du, de la, des)
- Pronouns (y, en, relative pronouns)
- Reflexive verbs (se laver, s'habiller)
- Negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais, ne...rien)
- Interrogatives (est-ce que, inversion)

### False Friends (French-English)

- Assister ≠ assist (to attend vs to help)
- Actuellement ≠ actually (currently vs actually)
- Librairie ≠ library (bookstore vs library)
- Rester ≠ rest (to stay vs to rest)
- Attendre ≠ attend (to wait vs to attend)

**Development Time:** 210-235 hours (higher due to complex grammar)
