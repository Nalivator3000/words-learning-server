# Italian → English Vocabulary Plan

**Target Audience:** English speakers learning Italian
**Difficulty Level:** Medium (easier than French/German for English speakers)
**Market Size:** Medium-Large (popular for travel, culture, cuisine)
**Priority:** MEDIUM

---

## Total Target: ~10,800 words

### Italian-Specific Features

1. **Gender System** - Masculine/Feminine (simpler than French)
2. **Pronunciation** - Phonetic (easier than French)
3. **Verb Conjugations** - Complex but regular patterns
4. **Articles** - Il, lo, la, i, gli, le + un, uno, una
5. **Subjunctive** - Congiuntivo (4 tenses)
6. **Double Consonants** - Pronunciation crucial (nonno vs nono)
7. **Formal/Informal** - Lei vs Tu
8. **Regional Variants** - Different dialects (focus on Standard Italian)

### Key Grammar Points by Level

**A1:**
- Present tense (-are, -ere, -ire verbs)
- Basic articles
- Gender recognition
- Numbers, days, months
- Essential phrases

**A2:**
- Past tense (passato prossimo)
- Imperfetto
- Direct/indirect pronouns
- Reflexive verbs
- Common irregulars

**B1:**
- Future tense
- Conditional
- Subjunctive introduction
- Relative pronouns
- Complex sentences

**B2:**
- Advanced subjunctive
- Passive voice
- Gerundio
- Formal language
- Idiomatic expressions

**C1-C2:**
- Literary Italian
- Professional terminology
- Regional variations
- Historical forms
- Sophisticated expressions

### Advantages for English Speakers

1. **Phonetic spelling** - Write as you hear
2. **Latin roots** - Many cognates with English
3. **Regular patterns** - More predictable than French
4. **Clear pronunciation** - Each letter usually one sound

### Common Cognates

- Famiglia → Family
- Musica → Music
- Teatro → Theater
- Universit à → University
- Importante → Important

### False Friends

- Camera ≠ camera (room vs camera)
- Parenti ≠ parents (relatives vs parents)
- Libreria ≠ library (bookstore vs library)
- Fattoria ≠ factory (farm vs factory)

### Cultural Vocabulary Focus

**A1-A2:**
- Food & cuisine (pasta, pizza, gelato, etc.)
- Family & relationships (la famiglia italiana)
- Greetings & politeness
- Travel & tourism

**B1-B2:**
- Art & architecture
- Fashion & design
- Music & opera
- Italian history & culture

**C1-C2:**
- Literature (Dante, Boccaccio, etc.)
- Film & cinema
- Political terminology
- Business Italian

### Example Entry Format

```
| Italian | English | Example | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| il libro | the book | Leggo il libro. | m., pl: i libri |
| parlare | to speak | Parlo italiano. | -are verb, regular |
| andare | to go | Vado a casa. | irregular, vado/vai/va |
| ciao | hi/bye | Ciao, come stai? | informal only |
```

### Level Breakdown

**A1:** 800 words (greetings, food, family, basics)
**A2:** 1,000 words (travel, shopping, hobbies)
**B1:** 1,200 words (work, education, culture)
**B2:** 1,800 words (politics, society, abstract concepts)
**C1:** 2,500 words (professional, academic)
**C2:** 3,500 words (literary, specialized)

### Priority Vocabulary Themes

1. **Food & Cuisine** - Major cultural element
2. **Art & Culture** - Italian heritage
