# Russian → English Vocabulary Plan

**Target Audience:** English speakers learning Russian
**Difficulty Level:** Very High (complex grammar, different alphabet)
**Market Size:** Medium-Large (strategic/cultural interest)
**Priority:** MEDIUM-HIGH

---

## Total Target: ~10,800 words

### Russian-Specific Challenges

1. **Cyrillic Alphabet** - 33 letters, completely different writing system
2. **Cases** - 6 grammatical cases (Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, Prepositional)
3. **Aspects** - Perfective vs Imperfective verbs
4. **Gender** - Masculine, Feminine, Neuter
5. **No Articles** - No equivalent to a/an/the
6. **Free Word Order** - Flexible syntax (SVO not fixed)
7. **Palatalization** - Soft vs hard consonants
8. **Animacy** - Affects accusative case
9. **Verbs of Motion** - Complex system (идти/ходить, ехать/ездить)

### Key Grammar Points by Level

**A1:**
- Cyrillic alphabet
- Basic case usage (Nominative, Accusative)
- Present tense verbs
- Gender recognition
- Numbers 1-100

**A2:**
- All 6 cases introduction
- Past and future tense
- Reflexive verbs
- Comparatives
- More complex case usage

**B1:**
- Aspect pairs (perfective/imperfective)
- Verbs of motion
- Participles introduction
- Complex sentences
- Prefixed verbs

**B2:**
- Advanced case usage
- Gerunds (деепричастия)
- Participles (причастия)
- Complex aspect usage
- Formal/business language

**C1-C2:**
- Literary Russian
- Complex syntax
- Professional terminology
- Stylistic variants
- Idiomatic expressions

### Common Difficulties for English Speakers

1. **Case system** - No equivalent in English
2. **Aspect** - Choosing perfective vs imperfective
3. **Pronunciation** - Stress patterns, reduction
4. **Verbs of motion** - Unidirectional vs multidirectional
5. **Gender** - Must memorize for each noun
6. **No "to be" in present** - Я студент (I student = I am a student)

### False Friends (Russian-English via transliteration)

- Magazin (магазин) ≠ magazine (store vs magazine)
- Fabric (фабрика) ≠ fabric (factory vs fabric)
- Intelligent (интеллигентный) ≠ intelligent (cultured vs intelligent)
- Accurate (аккуратный) ≠ accurate (neat vs accurate)

### Example Entry Format

```
| Russian | English | Example | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| книга | book | Я читаю книгу. | f., Acc: книгу |
| идти | to go (on foot, one direction) | Я иду в школу. | impf., see also ходить |
| говорить | to speak | Я говорю по-русски. | impf., pf: сказать |
```
